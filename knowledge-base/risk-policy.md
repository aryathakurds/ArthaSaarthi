# Risk Suitability Policy

## Conservative Customer
A conservative customer prefers capital safety and predictable returns.

Recommended focus:
- Emergency fund
- Savings account
- Fixed deposits
- Recurring deposits
- Insurance review

Avoid:
- High-risk products
- Unverified investment platforms
- Concentrated market exposure

## Balanced Customer
A balanced customer can accept moderate market risk after maintaining liquidity.

Recommended focus:
- Emergency fund first
- Mix of low-risk deposits and SIPs
- Goal-based investing
- Tax-saving review

Guardrail:
Market-linked investment should not reduce emergency liquidity.

## Aggressive Customer
An aggressive customer may accept higher market risk for long-term goals.

Recommended focus:
- SIPs
- Diversified mutual funds
- Long-term goal investing

Guardrail:
High-risk allocation is allowed only if emergency fund, debt burden, and income stability are acceptable.

## Loan Suitability
Before suggesting a loan, check:
- Monthly income
- Existing EMI
- Estimated new EMI
- EMI-to-income ratio
- Emergency fund status
- Purpose of loan

If the new EMI is higher than safe monthly surplus, the assistant should caution the customer.

## Platform Safety
The assistant should flag platforms that:
- Promise unusually high returns
- Are not verified in the bank knowledge base
- Ask users to transfer money outside verified channels
- Encourage urgency or guaranteed profit claims

The assistant should recommend avoiding unverified platforms and escalating to a bank advisor.

## Unusual Transaction Verification
If a transaction is unusual, the assistant should trigger step-up verification.

Risk signals:
- High amount
- New beneficiary
- After normal banking hours
- Outside customer’s spending pattern
- Device or location mismatch

Allowed actions:
- Approve after MPIN/biometric/OTP verification
- Block if customer denies
- Hold if no response
- Escalate to fraud team
